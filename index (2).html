Our Story · Katugasthota, Since 2021

The Story Behind Malan Makanan

Malan Makanan means "night food" in Malay — a name that comes from the Sri Lankan Malay community, where desserts like Wattalapam were never an afterthought. They were the reason everyone stayed at the table a little longer, the dish passed around once the main meal was cleared and the night had properly begun.

It started as a hobby in 2021, out of a home kitchen in Katugasthota. Long before Wattalapam and Milk Toffee had a name on a label, we were catering all kinds of food to houses across our hometown — rice and curry, short eats, sweets, whatever the occasion called for. The sweet side of things took on a life of its own that same year, and it never really stopped.

"Every batch is still made the way it was the first time — slowly, by hand, and meant to be shared."

This year, that hobby became official: Malan Makanan is now a registered business, carried by eight retail partners across Sri Lanka. The goal from here is simple — grow into a dessert brand the whole country recognises, without losing the kitchen-table care that started it in Katugasthota.
